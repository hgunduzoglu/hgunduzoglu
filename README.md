## About Me

I am a full stack software engineer with a degree in **Industrial Engineering** — a background that shapes how I approach software: with a focus on systems, processes, optimization, and measurable outcomes.

I enjoy working across the full product lifecycle: designing architecture, building APIs, developing interfaces, modeling data, deploying systems, and improving products based on real usage. My engineering background draws me toward problems where software meets decision-making — forecasting, optimization, capacity planning, and data-driven operations.

I am especially interested in backend architecture, SaaS products, distributed systems, applied AI, and privacy-aware software design. I also enjoy exploring cryptography-related concepts and understanding how systems work under the hood.

## Selected Work

### Kalis / Clinix

A SaaS platform for psychology clinics, designed around multi-tenant architecture, appointment management, customer management, staff roles, payment workflows, audit logging, and AI-assisted operations. Deployed in production and used by real customers.

**Focus:** SaaS architecture, multi-tenancy, RBAC, PostgreSQL, secure clinical workflows

---

### Consensus

A survey-based social platform focused on anonymous participation, demographic insights, regional heatmaps, voting integrity, and social interaction around public questions.

**Focus:** Anonymous participation, social product design, device-level integrity, backend architecture

---

### Warehouse Process Improvement Decision Support System

A data-driven decision support system for e-commerce warehouse operations, covering demand forecasting, ABC classification, replenishment planning, and operational optimization. Built directly at the intersection of Industrial Engineering and software.

**Focus:** Forecasting, operations research, inventory control, warehouse process improvement

---

### Privacy-Preserving Social Verification

Experiments around privacy-preserving social account verification, where users can prove ownership or attributes without exposing unnecessary private data.

**Focus:** Privacy, verification protocols, cryptography-related concepts, decentralized identity
